IEEE International Conference on Data Mining 2023.

Half-day on December 4th, 2023, Shanghai,China

INTRODUCTION

For years, machine learning, or learning from examples, has advanced artificial intelligence (AI) to build systems capable of generating models from data or sensor signals without direct instruction. As data are becoming rapidly available in all fields, learning-enabled systems, which have embedded learning components in the core, are becoming increasingly popular and powerful in industry and our daily lives. Indeed, learning has the advantage of examining a large quantify of data to find subtle features, which are often beyond human’s capabilities and limitations. Nevertheless, as learning algorithms are often driven by overly simplified objective functions (or optimization goals) and the data collected for learning are frequently biased, incomplete, or even hazardous, the transition of relying on machine leaning models for decision making is potentially risky. This is mainly attributed to the facts that existing learning algorithms or models are very limited in terms of their adaptability to changing environments, reliability in handling unfamiliar or unknown circumstances, and responsibility of making decisions.

The first international workshop on Adaptable, Reliable, and Responsible Learning (ARRL) provides a venue to bring together researchers and practitioners to report recent advancement in addressing the three key aspects of learning: adaptability, reliability, and responsibility. The purpose is to investigate theoretical foundations, algorithm designs, and frameworks to ensure that our future learning-enabled systems to be adaptable: the learning is adaptive to changing environments and task requirements; reliable: the learning can provide statistical guarantees when being used in the field; and responsible: the learning is fair, explainable, and trustable.

We invite submissions that focus on recent advances in research/development of adaptable, reliable, and responsible (ARR) learning algorithms and designs. We also welcome papers discussing resources or software packages developed to tackle the ARR challenges, if the submissions have significant value to benefit others in the community.

Thus, there is a rising and urgent need towards Sustainable AI for both AI research and industry. Specifically, for sustainability of AI, we aim to reduce carbon emissions and huge computing power consumption, as well as addressing AI-related ethical issues, via developing advanced AI technology. This workshop aims to prompt state-of-the-art approaches on sustainable AI research, and also propagate data/resource-efficient methods and possible applications in the Sustainability domain. For example, submissions that are at the intersection of data mining and society like use of AI and data mining for ensuring affordable and clean energy and motivating climate/climate-change related action will be particularly welcome. The organizers invite researchers to participate and submit their research papers in the Sustainable AI Workshop.

TOPICS

Theory, methodology, and resource papers are welcome from any of the following areas, including but not limited to:


Adaptable Learning
  • Online/incremental learning
  • Transfer learning and domain adaptation
  • Lifelong/Continual Learning
  • Learning from heterogeneous and disparity data
  • Streaming data learning
  • Learning with Rejection/Abstain
  • Open-world learning (learning in unexpected/unknown environments)
Reliable Learning
  • Statistical reliability and measurement of robustness in learning
  • Level of confidence in learning-enabled systems
  • Robustness of learning in operating environments
  • Learning in adversarial environments
  • Learning with very few samples
  • Learning with Uncertainty Quantification
Responsible Learning
  • Explainable learning modules and architectures
  • Interpretability of learning results
  • Fairness of the learning and prediction
  • Trustworthiness and robustness of the learning
  • Privacy preserving and measurement in learning

Short bio of the organizers

Dr. Xingquan Zhu

Xingquan Zhu is a Full Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Florida Atlantic University, Boca Raton, FL, USA. His research interests include data mining, machine learning, and bioinformatics. Since 2000, he has published more than 300 refereed journal and conference papers in these areas, including four Best Paper Awards (PAKDD-21, IRI-18, PAKDD-13, ICTAI-05) and three Best Student Paper Awards (ICDM-20, ICKG-20, ICPR-12). He is the Program Committee Co-Chair for the 22nd IEEE International Conference on Data Mining (ICDM-2022), General Co-chair for the 2021 IEEE International Conference on Big Data (IEEE BigData-2021), and Program Committee Co-Chair for the 33rd International Conference on Scientific and Statistical Database Management (SSDBM-2021). He previously served as an associate editor of the IEEE Trans. on Knowledge and Data Engineering (2008-2012, 2014-2021), and currently serves as an associate editor of the ACM Trans. on Knowledge Discovery from Data (2017 - date), International Journal of Social Network Analysis and Mining SNAM (2010-date), Journal of Big Data (2013-date), and Network Modeling Analysis in Health Informatics and Bioinformatics Journal (2014-date).
